RiskCounts Approach to Residual Risk Ratings calculation

  1. Inherent Risk: Let us assume that inherent risk rating is (5,4) i.e., Risk Score is 5 X 4 = 20, VERY HIGH on the RiskHeatmap
  2. Control Rating: Let us assume that the control rating is confirmed by Risk Management at 6 (we should allow one decimal place at least, as seen why below) after reviewing Business Unit Head ratings, and any other risk data i.e., 60% control effectiveness is achieved
  3. Residual Risk:
    1. Residual Severity: Control rating below 50% does not impact the Severity Rating. i.e., benefit of Controls is given only to any effectiveness exceeding 50%. In such cases, the Residual Severity improves (is lower) by the amount of control improvement over 50%.

      So, Residual Severity (X-axis value) – is derived as follows:
      1. where 0 < Control Rating < 50%, Residual Severity=Inherent Severity
      2. where 50 < Control Rating < 100%,
        {{"Residual Severity = {Inherent Severity * (100 - (Control Rating-50))%} "}}
        In the above examples, Residual Severity = 5 * (100 – (60-50))% = 4.5
    2. Residual Likelihood derives all the benefit of controls
      {{"So Residual Likelihood (Y axis) is derived as {4 * (100-60)%} = 1.6"}}
      So, the Residual Risk rating is now (4.5, 1.6), for a risk-score of 7.2
